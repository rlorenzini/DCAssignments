Common Interactions with Officers

Officers love to play Twenty Questions and use technical words.

Interacting with the police can be intimidating.

Yet an officer is just a person doing a job. They want the same respect and courtesy you'd show anyone else.

When will I interact with an Officer?

If you call 911, are inside a business which calls 911, or are witness to a possible crime or accident, an officer will ask you questions regarding what you may have seen.

There is no reason to become alarmed when an officer approaches you.

A routine reaction may go as follows:

  • "My name is Officer Lorenzini and I am with the Cobb County Police Department."
  • "I need to ask you a few questions regarding the recent incident."
  • "What is your name/address/phone number? May I see your ID?"

Why does an officer need my Driver's License/State ID?

Officers run names all day long. By using a government issued identification card, not only does it save the officer time and effort, it allows them to retrieve your information later if needed.

Until an officer can identify you in the system, you will be detained.

Would you rather provide your ID or wait for an officer to find you BY NAME? How many people share your name?

Using an ID also allows other officers listening over the radio to run your information for the officer.

As you can probably see, this helps an officer in many ways.

An officer asking for an ID does not imply you are guilty of anything.
