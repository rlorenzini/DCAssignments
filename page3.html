"I am going to detain you in handcuffs."

Hold the @$#%ing phone. Did that OFFICER just say he is ARRESTING me?!

In short, no. The officer is still DETAINING you, which I covered under the DETAINED page.

Being detained means you are involved in an investigation of a possible crime, whether that is...

  • Traffic Violation
  • Domestic Situation
  • 911 Phone Call
  • Victim of a Crime

But why the handcuffs?

Let me give you a very common situation which might lead an officer to detain someone in handcuffs.

The 911 Call Center received a phone call. The call taker hears screaming in the background before the call drops.

Modern Call Centers can track where a call originated from if it is associated with an address.

The officer arrives at the address; let's say it is a residence.

While approaching the house, the officer hears screaming.

A woman runs out of the front door with a man chasing her, screaming for her to "get back here".

What do you think is happening? Is this a volatile situation? Is anyone acting in a threatening manner?

This is a common domestic situation officers find themselves in. They have very little information, almost next to no context, and they must make a decision right then and there.

What decision would YOU make?

During my time as an officer, I would approach this situation as follows:

Separate the two, but keep both of them within my line of sight.

Have the two stand or sit in an area I have checked and know is free of weapons.

That means no kitchen (knives), garages (tools), or couches (weapons under cushions), unless I know the area is free of weapons.

I proceed to talk to one at a time, while another officer is with the other individual (if possible).

But how did it come to pulling out handcuffs?

If one of the individuals keeps acting aggressively and will not obey the officer's commands, the officer must determine if there is a safety risk.

If the officer believes there is a safety risk, or someone may become violent, the officer will detain everyone in handcuffs until he knows the full extent of the situation.

Once the officer has completed their investigation, he will release everyone from handcuffs UNLESS they have determined a crime has been committed.

Common situations where you'll be detained in handcuffs.

  • If Drugs are Involved
  • If Weapons are Readily Available
  • If Physical Violence is Being Threatened
  • If Both Parties have Physical Injuries, but the Aggressor has not been Determined

I carry a gun legally in my car. I don't want to be handcuffed for legally carrying a gun!

You shouldn't be, and very likely will not be, unless you give the officer a reason to suspect you're a threat to yourself or others.

Detaining in handcuffs is a measure to de-escalate a volatile situation before Use of Force is necessary.

If an officer says he is putting you in handcuffs during his investigation, it is best to comply and not resist.
