The author of this website, Richard Lorenzini, is a former police officer for the Cobb County, Georgia Police Department. Richard regularly uses his knowledge, training, and experience to help others understand their basic rights, how to approach police officers, and what to do when interacting with law enforcement agents.

 

Terminology

  • Law Enforcement Officer: Any person within a government agency granted authority to uphold the law, make arrests, and detain individuals during investigations.
  • Jurisdiction: The boundaries an officer has granted authority over: state; county; city; federal.
  • Sworn: The authority to uphold laws has been granted to a person within a specific jurisdiction.
  • Deputized: The authority to uphold all laws within all states of the United States has been granted to a person.
  • Reasonable Suspicion: Using the present facts, an OFFICER can articulate a potential crime, or that a person is armed and dangerous. Reasonable suspicion is needed to detain or pat down.
  • Pat Down: Also known as a "frisk". When an officer checks a person for weapons in a non-invasive manner.
  • Search: When an officer arrests a person, he checks the person for all personal items and possible contraband, weapons, or tools of the crime.
  • Probable Cause: Given the totality of the circumstances, any REASONABLE person would come to the same conclusion. Probable cause is necessary for an arrest, and is used by juries during court trials.
  • Miranda Rights: During an interrogation, people have individual rights, which MUST be told to them by the interrogating officer.
  • Investigation: When an officer is looking into whether or not a crime was committed, the alleged area and people involved are part of his investigation. Until the officer has removed all suspicion of a crime, the investigation continues and everyone possible involved in considered detained.
  • Interrogation: When an officer intends to question you about a specific crime. For an interrogation to be conducted, the officer must have probable cause and have taken you into custody (arrested you).
  • Detained: During an investigation, or when there is reasonable suspicion you are involved in a crime, you are NOT free to leave until the investigating officer has dispelled all suspicion.
  • Use of Force: The standard an officer is held to when gaining compliance from a person.
  • Excessive Force: When an officer uses one level of force for longer than is necessary, refuses to try another level of force, or exceeds an acceptable escalation of use of force.
  • Suspect: Any person possibly involved in a crime.
  • Victim: Any person, business, or the jurisdiction which is the target of a crime. The jurisdiction is the victim of traffic violations.
  • Miranda Rights: Your legal rights which must be read to you before an interrogation.
